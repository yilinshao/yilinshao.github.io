Yilin Shao

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science and Technology at Xidian University, advised by Prof. Licheng Jiao at the Key Laboratory of Intelligent Perception and Image Understanding. Earlier I worked on semantic segmentation, remote sensing interpretation, and multimodal foundation models. I received my M.S. in Control Science and Engineering (2022) and my B.S. in Communication Engineering (2019), both from Xidian University.

My research interests span 4D scene generation, causal representation learning, and physically interactive 3D content creation. In the short term, I aim to build compositional 3D scenes that are semantically consistent and physically plausible. In the long term, I seek to create physically interactive 4D scenes that respond to arbitrary, previously unseen inputs in a causally controllable way.

Research

I'm interested in 3D/4D computer vision, generative models, and causal representation learning. My recent work centers on compositional and physically consistent 3D scene generation.

Penetration-Free Compositional 3D Generation via Gaussian Surface Offset
Yilin Shao, Licheng Jiao, Lingling Li, Xu Liu, Fang Liu, Wenping Ma, Long Sun, Jiaxuan Zhao
ECCV, 2026
pdf / code

A Gaussian surface offset that mimics real-world repulsive forces resolves inter-object penetrations, yielding complete, semantically consistent, penetration-free compositional 3D scenes.

CoT: Contourlet Transformer for Hierarchical Semantic Segmentation
Yilin Shao, Long Sun, Licheng Jiao, Xu Liu, Fang Liu, Lingling Li, Shuyuan Yang
TNNLS, 2024
IEEE Xplore / code

A Transformer–CNN hybrid that uses the contourlet transform to model fine-grained structure, achieving state-of-the-art hierarchical semantic segmentation with strong robustness to image corruption.

Which Target to Focus on: Class-Perception for Semantic Segmentation of Remote Sensing
Long Sun, Lingling Li, Yilin Shao, Licheng Jiao, Xu Liu, Puhua Chen, Fang Liu, Shuyuan Yang, Biao Hou
TGRS, 2023
IEEE Xplore

An entropy-guided module steers computation toward the most uncertain boundary points, yielding sharper remote-sensing segmentation without extra overhead.
